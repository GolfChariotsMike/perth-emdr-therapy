About the Therapy

What is EMDR?

Eye Movement Desensitisation and Reprocessing (EMDR) is an evidence-based psychotherapy approach that has been extensively researched and proven effective for treating trauma and PTSD. Recommended by the World Health Organisation (WHO), EMDR helps the brain process traumatic memories that have become 'stuck', reducing their emotional intensity and allowing for healing and integration.
🧠

How It Works

EMDR uses bilateral stimulation (eye movements, taps or tones) to help the brain reprocess distressing memories at a neurological level.

⏱️

Session Structure

Sessions are 50–90 minutes. Initial sessions focus on history-taking, preparation and building coping resources before processing begins.

🏆

WHO Endorsed

EMDR is on the WHO's list of recommended treatments for trauma. It's one of the most researched psychotherapy approaches in the world.
